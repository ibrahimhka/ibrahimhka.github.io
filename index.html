Lead Story

A Decade of Production Engineering

From banking infrastructure to cloud migrations, frameworks, and agentic development — the throughline is code that ships and stays shipped.

IBRAHIM HOBEIKA has spent twelve years writing production software across finance, healthcare, and AI — building systems in high-throughput environments, managing Kubernetes clusters that span clouds, and designing platforms that let other engineers deploy without friction. The work is grounded in infrastructure as code, event-driven architectures, and the confidence that comes from running production at scale.

More recently, that same engineering discipline has turned toward agentic systems — leveraging the B.Y.O.A. (Bring Your Own Agent) pattern to build meeting infrastructure where humans and AI models coordinate in realtime, and agentic isolation and execution to build quantitative research platforms that apply statistical rigour to strategy generation. The tooling changes; the approach does not: ship, observe, iterate. The hardest problems remain organisational, and the best architecture is the one that lets teams move independently.
